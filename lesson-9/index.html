Short Bio Sadie Sink

Sadie Elizabeth Sink (born April 16, 2002) is an American actress. She began her career in theater, playing the title role in the musical Annie (2012–14) and young Elizabeth II in the historical play The Audience (2015) on Broadway. In 2016, she made her film debut in the biographical sports drama Chuck. Sink had her breakthrough portraying Max Mayfield in the Netflix science fiction series Stranger Things (2017–2025) and received critical praise for her performance in its fourth season. Sink appeared in the horror film trilogy Fear Street in 2021 and starred in Darren Aronofsky's 2022 psychological drama The Whale, for which she received a nomination for the Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Young Performer. She earned a Tony Award nomination for Best Actress in a Play for her performance in the Broadway production of John Proctor Is the Villain (2025).

Early life

Sadie Elizabeth Sink[1][2] was born in Brenham, Texas on April 16, 2002.[3][4] She has English, German, and Irish ancestry.[5] Her mother Lori (née Baughman) is a math teacher, and her father Casey Sink is a football coach.[6] She has three older brothers, Mitchell, Caleb, and Spencer, and a younger sister, Jacey, who plays younger versions of her characters in Stranger Things 3 and The Whale.[7][8] While her family was sports-oriented, she and her brother Mitchell were interested in performing arts, particularly musical theater.[3][9] The pair would often recreate scenes from High School Musical (2006) and watch Broadway plays and Tony Award performances.[10][3][11] When Sink was seven, her mother put her in acting classes in Houston.[12] Sink began acting in community theater with a production of The Best Christmas Pageant Ever in Brenham at age seven.[3] When she was eight years old, she had a leading role in a local production of The Secret Garden, which involved "more learning lines and real practice". The experience encouraged her to pursue a professional acting career.[3][12] In 2012, Sink's family supported her career by moving to New Jersey.[13][14] She began homeschooling while in second grade and returned to regular school after performing in The Audience (2015).[15][16]

Career

Her early professional career began in theater. As a child, she performed on Broadway in productions such as Annie and later in The Audience, where she acted alongside Helen Mirren. Performing on Broadway helped her gain early experience and recognition as a young actress. Sadie’s international breakthrough came in 2017 when she joined the Netflix series Stranger Things. She played the character Max Mayfield, a skateboard-loving and strong-willed teenager who quickly became one of the most popular characters in the show. Her performance, especially in the fourth season, was widely praised for its emotional depth. In addition to television, Sadie also built a film career. She starred in the horror movie Fear Street Part Two: 1978, part of a Netflix trilogy based on books by R. L. Stine. She later appeared in the drama film The Whale, where she acted alongside Brendan Fraser. The film received significant critical attention and won major awards. Another project that brought her attention was All Too Well: The Short Film, directed by Taylor Swift. Sadie played the lead role in this emotional short film, which was connected to Swift’s song All Too Well. Throughout her career, Sadie Sink has been known for strong dramatic performances and the ability to portray emotionally complex characters. Even though she started acting as a child, she successfully transitioned into more mature film and television roles. Today she is considered one of the most promising young actresses of her generation, continuing to work in major film and television projects while also maintaining connections to theater.
